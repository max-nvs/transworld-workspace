Hey [Chairman],

I looked into the workspace idea you suggested and I'm happy to say it's very doable. Here's what I've mapped out.

**The approach:**

We'll build a central workspace that sits in front of all our internal tools. It won't replace anything we've already built — the existing tools stay exactly as they are. The workspace just becomes the one front door everyone walks through.

**How it will work for the team:**
- Everyone gets one account and one login
- After logging in, they land on a dashboard showing the tools available to them
- What each person sees depends on their role — so not everyone sees everything, just what's relevant to them
- They click a tool and they're straight in, no second login required

**Where we stand:**
- Two of our tools are already built and running
- Two more are in development
- This is actually the perfect time to put the workspace in place — the two new tools can be built to work with it from day one, and we'll connect the existing two without needing to rebuild them

**What this will give us:**
- One place to manage who has access to what
- Easy to add new tools down the road — they just show up on the dashboard
- Cleaner experience for the team, no more scattered logins
- Better visibility into who's using what

I've already started working through the technical side of how to build this. I'm confident we can get this done without disrupting anything that's already working.

I'll keep you posted as things move forward. Let me know if you have any questions or anything you'd want done differently.
